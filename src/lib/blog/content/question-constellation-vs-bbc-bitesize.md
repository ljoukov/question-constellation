## Quick comparison

| Feature        | Question Constellation                                       | BBC Bitesize                                                    |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Main use       | Public GCSE questions with answer chains and mark checklists | Free curriculum explainers, revision guides, videos and quizzes |
| Strength       | Exam-answer structure and transfer between question families | Accessible explanations for first learning and revision         |
| Question depth | Focused on exam-style answering and mark-scheme language     | Good for topic checks, with lighter answer repair               |
| Best moment    | When a student needs to practise how to answer               | When a student needs to understand or refresh the topic         |
| Best fit       | Students preparing for mark-scored answers                   | Students building the knowledge base before exam practice       |

## Understanding comes first

BBC Bitesize is useful because it gives students approachable explanations. If a student is lost on the topic, that kind of public guide can be the right first step.

Question Constellation starts once the student is ready to meet the exam question. The product asks what marks the answer needs and which chain carries those marks.

## Why GCSE practice needs another layer

Many students can read a topic page and understand it. The exam then changes the wording, adds data, or asks for an explanation in a new context.

That is where answer chains help. They give the student a way to rebuild the response rather than depend on memory of one page.

## Best way to use both

Use Bitesize to understand the topic. Then use Question Constellation to practise a real question and check the answer against a mark chain.

## Verdict

BBC Bitesize is a strong free explainer. Question Constellation is a focused practice layer for students who need to turn understanding into marks.
