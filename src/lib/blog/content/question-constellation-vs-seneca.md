## Quick comparison

| Feature           | Question Constellation                                             | Seneca                                                                |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Main use          | Exam-question atlas organized by reusable answer chains            | Interactive revision courses with short explanations and quick checks |
| Learning sequence | Question, chain, mark checklist, constellation, practice           | Course section, explanation, quiz interaction, progress tracking      |
| Best for          | Students who know the content but need exam technique and transfer | Students who want guided topic revision across a course               |
| Feedback focus    | Shows the missing reasoning link in an answer                      | Shows whether the learner answered a course check correctly           |
| Good pairing      | Use after a course lesson to practise exam answers                 | Use before question practice to refresh topic knowledge               |

## Different entry points

Seneca is organized like a course. That makes it useful for students who want to work through topic content and quick checks.

Question Constellation is organized like an exam atlas. The entry point is a question, then the student opens the chain and practises related questions.

## Why this matters for GCSE answers

Course revision can help a student remember content. GCSE marks often require more than recall. The student has to choose the right facts, order them, and connect them to the command word.

Answer chains make that structure visible.

## Best way to use both

Use Seneca for a course pass through the topic. Use Question Constellation for question practice, weak-answer improvement and transfer.

## Verdict

Seneca is a course-style revision product. Question Constellation is a question-style practice product. The second becomes more useful when the student already has enough topic knowledge to attempt exam answers.
