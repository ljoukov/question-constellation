## Quick comparison

| Student job    | Question Constellation                                                                 | Medly AI                                                                                                 |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Main use       | Public GCSE question pages, answer chains, mark checklists and transfer practice       | AI tutoring, adaptive practice, AI marking, upload-and-mark workflows and mock-exam style practice       |
| Best moment    | When a student knows the topic but keeps missing links in written answers              | When a student wants a broader AI study workspace that teaches, questions and marks                      |
| Learning style | Question first, then reveal the reusable chain behind the marks                        | Tutor first or practice first, with adaptive recommendations and answer feedback                         |
| Search fit     | Narrow pages for specific GCSE questions, chains and past-paper style revision queries | Broader product pages and app workflows for students already looking for an AI exam-preparation platform |
| Best fit       | Students who want to see why several exam questions use the same answer logic          | Students who want one AI-heavy workspace for lessons, questions, marking, mocks and progress tracking    |

## What Medly AI is built for

Medly AI's public pages describe a larger exam-preparation platform. Its comparison pages talk about AI tutoring, adaptive learning, thousands of exam-style questions, handwriting support, upload-and-mark, mock exams and analytics.

That is useful when the student wants a full study environment. The product promise reaches beyond "find a question". It is closer to "teach me, question me, mark me and decide what I should do next".

## What Question Constellation is built for

Question Constellation is deliberately narrower. It starts with a concrete GCSE question and asks a smaller question: what chain of reasoning earns the marks?

That matters for students who know the topic but write incomplete answers. A weak answer may contain the first idea, then skip the steps that examiners reward. The answer chain makes those missing links visible, then shows nearby questions where the same chain appears again.

## Where the choice changes

If a student cannot explain a topic at all, a tutoring platform can be the better first stop. The student may need teaching, examples and repeated questioning before exam practice is productive.

If a student can explain the topic but still loses marks, Question Constellation becomes more useful. The student sees the mark checklist, the common missing link and the same thinking pattern in another question.

## Using both without wasting time

A practical workflow is simple:

1. Use a tutor or content resource to study the topic.
2. Open a Question Constellation page for a real GCSE question.
3. Try the answer before reading the chain.
4. Reveal the chain and compare it with the mark checklist.
5. Practise another question that uses the same chain.

This keeps the two jobs separate: study the content, then improve the exam answer.

## Verdict

Medly AI is a broader AI exam-preparation platform. Question Constellation is a more focused GCSE question atlas built around answer chains and mark-scheme structure.

For a student who wants an AI tutor, adaptive practice and marking in one place, Medly AI is closer to that job. For a student who wants fast public pages that explain how exam answers transfer across questions, Question Constellation is the more direct tool.
