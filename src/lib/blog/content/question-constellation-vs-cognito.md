## Quick comparison

| Feature           | Question Constellation                                                                      | Cognito                                                                               |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Main use          | Real GCSE questions grouped by answer chains, mark checklists and transfer practice         | Video lessons, quizzes, exam questions and past papers for science and maths revision |
| Best moment       | When a student can revise content but loses marks in written answers                        | When a student needs a clear first explanation or a lesson refresh                    |
| Question practice | Starts from exam-style questions and shows the reasoning chain behind the marks             | Provides topic practice and past-paper access alongside lessons                       |
| Feedback style    | Mark checklist plus common missing links, with optional answer checking on supported routes | Quiz and practice feedback, with the lesson video as the main teaching object         |
| Best fit          | Students who need exam-answer structure after they know the content                         | Students who want structured science and maths explanations before practice           |

## Where Question Constellation fits

Question Constellation starts with a real GCSE-style question. The page then shows the answer chain, the short sequence of reasoning links that turns a weak answer into a mark-scoring answer.

That matters when a student already understands the topic but writes answers that jump too quickly. A biology question may need a chain such as blood flow, oxygen, respiration, energy, effect. A physics question may need force, acceleration, velocity change, resultant effect. The wording changes, but the chain keeps the answer complete.

## Where Cognito fits

Cognito is useful when the student needs a clean explanation before exam practice. Short lessons, quizzes and topic resources can build the knowledge base that later answers depend on.

If the student cannot yet explain the content, Cognito may be the better first stop. If the student can explain the content but misses the exam links, Question Constellation is designed for that later step.

## Best way to use both

Use Cognito to study or refresh the topic. Then use Question Constellation to practise a question, reveal the chain, rewrite the answer and try a related transfer question.

## Verdict

Cognito is a strong science and maths study resource. Question Constellation is more focused: exam questions, answer chains, mark checklists and transfer practice. The best choice depends on whether the student needs topic teaching or exam-answer improvement.
