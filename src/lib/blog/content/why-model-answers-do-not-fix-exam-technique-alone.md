## The fluency trap

A polished model answer can feel easy to understand. That feeling is useful, but it can hide the harder task: producing a similar answer under exam conditions.

Students need to know which links in the answer carry marks. Without that, they may copy the style of the model answer while missing the logic.

## What to add beside a model answer

The model answer should sit beside a checklist and a chain. The checklist says what the examiner needed. The chain says how the ideas connect.

A common weak answer is also valuable. It shows the student the mistake they are likely to make, such as naming a process without explaining the gradient, energy source or effect.

- Model answer for the complete response.
- Checklist for mark coverage.
- Answer chain for transferable structure.
- Weak-answer note for the common missing link.

## Why transfer is the test

If a student can only reproduce the model answer for one question, the revision has limited value. If the student can use the chain in a related question, the technique is more durable.

That is why Question Constellation points students from one question to a constellation rather than leaving them with a single answer.

## A better way to read model answers

Before reading the model answer, try the question. After reading it, cover it and write the chain from memory. Then attempt a related question with the chain hidden.
