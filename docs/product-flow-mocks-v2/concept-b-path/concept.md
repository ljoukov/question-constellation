# Concept B тАФ The Next Stretch

## Product thesis

Question Constellation becomes a calm, finite journey through Maya's taught GCSE scope. The home
screen does not ask her to configure a session or interpret a dashboard. It answers one question:
**what is the best useful thing I can do in the next ten minutes?**

The interface uses a restrained path metaphor: a fine route line, editorial waypoints, and one
raised тАЬnext stretchтАЭ card. It behaves like the contents and margin marks of a well-designed revision
guide, not a game map. Completed practice sits behind the current point, the next question is in
focus, and material school has not covered sits ahead in a quiet тАЬlater at schoolтАЭ region. Distance
along the path means order, not ability.

The distinctive product objectтАФthe answer chainтАФstill has to be earned. Maya starts with a concrete
exam question. After her attempt, the marked links appear as a method, and the path continues to a
different question that needs the same causal reasoning.

## Visual and interaction system

- Warm paper canvas, near-black green ink, forest-green actions, muted ochre for the current
  waypoint, and cool blue-grey for тАЬlater at schoolтАЭ. No neon, trophies, streaks, mascots, or game
  terrain.
- A British editorial type hierarchy: sturdy serif for exam prompts and method statements; neutral
  sans serif for navigation, metadata, controls, and feedback.
- Thin path rules and numbered waypoints orient the journey. Cards remain rectangular, printable,
  and practical. The question is always the most visually interesting object.
- Desktop uses a narrow route margin beside a single main surface. Mobile turns the same route into
  a vertical line above and below the active card; nothing depends on hover or a wide canvas.
- Explore/practise pages are warm and inviting. Authentic paper mode removes the path art and uses a
  white, ruled, paper-like exam surface.

## Complete route and journey

### 0. Public acquisition remains question-first

A search, teacher, or short-form link still lands on a public, indexable question page. Board,
qualification, subject, tier, paper, source reference, marks, and whether the item is authentic,
adapted, or generated are visible. `Try without hints` and `Show answer chain` work without sign-in.
Signing in only adds the attempted question to Maya's path; it never precedes value.

### 1. Signed-in home: one next stretch

The main route is the signed-in home (`/`). Its first viewport contains:

- `Good afternoon, Maya` and the compact state `14 checked ┬╖ 7 recall due`;
- one dominant card headed `Your next 10 minutes`;
- the real 4-mark Biology prompt: `Explain why heart attack survivors get out of breath easily when
they exercise gently.`;
- full trust metadata: `AQA ┬╖ GCSE Combined Science ┬╖ Higher ┬╖ Biology Paper 1 ┬╖ November 2021 ┬╖
Q5.5 ┬╖ 4 marks ┬╖ authentic`;
- the action `Start 4-mark question` and the explanation link `Why this?`;
- a short rationale: `You usually know the facts. This checks the oxygen тЖТ respiration тЖТ energy link
that sometimes drops out of your longer answers.`

The path margin shows only meaningful adjacent state: recently checked behind, this question now,
and `7 recall due` next. A calm end-cap says `Later at school: Homeostasis ┬╖ Inheritance ┬╖ Ecology`
and `Waiting, not overdue`. This finite alternative replaces an infinite recommendation feed.

`Why this?` opens a small anchored sheet with the evidence and selection rule, never a chat:
`Organisation is in your covered scope; this takes about 8 minutes; it uses no questions from your
reserved 2024 paper; your last two explanations skipped the energy link.`

### 2. Question destination: attempt before reveal

`Start 4-mark question` opens the normal paper-like question route. The route margin reads
`Close this gap ┬╖ 1 of 3`, but the content area is an uncluttered exam question. The answer chain,
model answer, and marks are hidden until Maya acts. The only primary action is `Check answer`, with
`Use mark checklist` as the static alternative. Checking is an explicit, optional model action;
without a model the same surface uses the curated checklist.

On mobile, the metadata wraps into two readable lines, the answer field fills the width, and the
sticky action area contains only those two actions. The stable shell and explicit question-paper
aspect regions prevent loading shifts.

### 3. Gap result: evidence, then one gap-closing rewrite

The result is not a score bubble. It states `3 of 4 links found` and highlights evidence from Maya's
own answer against the checklist:

1. damaged heart pumps less blood тАФ found;
2. less oxygen reaches cells тАФ found;
3. less aerobic respiration тАФ found;
4. less energy is released, so breathing increases to compensate тАФ missing.

The feedback says: `You connected oxygen to respiration. Add what happens to energy release, then
link that to getting out of breath.` The single next action is `Rewrite to close the gap`. A passed
link stays recognized on the retry; the goal does not move.

### 4. Method and transfer: continue the path

After a successful rewrite, the product names the earned method in adult, exam-facing language:

`reduced delivery тЖТ less oxygen тЖТ less aerobic respiration тЖТ less energy тЖТ symptom or compensation`

The next card is a genuinely new context from the same curated chain: a shortened damaged-alveoli
question about why one person breathes faster during exercise (`AQA ┬╖ Combined Science ┬╖ Higher ┬╖
Biology Paper 1 ┬╖ June 2024 ┬╖ Q6.3 ┬╖ 4 marks`). Because the board removes the source figure and
condenses its setup, it is labelled `stretch ┬╖ adapted from AQA ┬╖ same method`, not presented as an
unaltered authentic item or a new abstract taxonomy.

Continuation is a compact control attached to this one card:

- `Another like this` keeps topic and difficulty;
- `Harder` advances to a stretch or exam-transfer member of the same chain;
- `Different topic` keeps the session length and chooses from covered scope;
- `Not covered yet` removes the topic from recommendations and offers to update taught scope;
- `Why this?` exposes the source, transfer distance, and evidence used to select it.

Choosing an option replaces the next card in place. It does not append an endless feed.

### 5. Recall launch: one choice, then start

The `7 recall due` waypoint opens `/recall` in launch state rather than the dense configuration
surface. It already knows `Biology`, `covered topics only`, and `7 due ┬╖ about 6 minutes`.

Maya makes at most one choiceтАФ`Flashcards` or `Multiple choice`тАФthen taps `Start 7-card review`.
The first-card preview is the real prompt `What is the function of ribosomes?` A quiet `Change
scope` link opens advanced controls only on demand. Session size, due ordering, subject, and covered
scope are sensible defaults, not mandatory form fields.

### 6. Progress: answer a student question, then permit drill-down

`My path` opens progress with a high-level answer first:

- `Current evidence: around 7тАУ8`, explicitly described as cautious rather than predicted;
- `Evidence strength: moderate ┬╖ 14 checked questions`;
- `Assessed: 4 of 7 Biology topic areas`; and
- `Current focus: keep the oxygen тЖТ respiration тЖТ energy link in longer explanations`.

The curriculum route is divided into `Covered and assessed`, `Covered, more evidence needed`, and
`Later at school`. The last region is blue-grey and says `Not included in your grade range yet`.
It never uses red, a low percentage, an empty progress bar, or тАЬbehindтАЭ. `See official AQA scope`
drills into specification references, question counts, evidence dates, and confidence. Available
question counts are labelled as bank availability, never curriculum coverage.

### 7. Adjust taught scope without curriculum browsing

`Change covered scope` opens a compact sheet from home, recall, progress, or `Not covered yet`.
It begins with the current interpretation rather than a syllabus tree:

- Covered at school: Cell Biology; Organisation; Infection and Response; Bioenergetics.
- Later at school: Homeostasis and Response; Inheritance, Variation and Evolution; Ecology.

Copy directly states: `Not yet covered is not a low score. We will keep these off your path until
you add them.` Maya can toggle a topic and save without changing board, tier, or paper settings.

An optional command field controls these same settings. For example:
`We have not covered ecology. Give me ten minutes on Biology and avoid my reserved paper.`
Before anything important changes, a structured confirmation shows:
`Biology ┬╖ 10 minutes ┬╖ Ecology: later at school ┬╖ 2024 mock: protected`. Maya confirms or edits the
chips. This is a command palette, not a tutoring conversation.

### 8. Authentic paper mode: protect freshness

`Past papers` includes a distinct `Fresh mock` section. The 2024 AQA Combined Science Higher Biology
Paper 1 tile is marked `Reserved ┬╖ unseen` with the consequence in plain language. `Start fresh mock`
opens the paper with timing and no answer access. `Use 2023 practice paper instead` preserves the
reservation.

Any action that would reveal a reserved question, mark scheme, model answer, or guided hint triggers
one confirmation: `Reveal this reserved paper? It will be marked exposed and will no longer count as
a fresh mock.` The destructive action is `Reveal and mark exposed`; the safe action is `Keep it
reserved`. Paper states are visible and literal: `Reserved (unseen)`, `Exposed (questions or answers
seen)`, and `Attempted`.

## Deliberate exclusions

- No equal-weight grid of subjects or tools on signed-in home.
- No infinite recommendation feed, celebratory game map, streak, XP, rank, or тАЬlevelтАЭ.
- No exposed answer-chain taxonomy before a concrete attempt.
- No chatbot home, tutoring transcript, or separate conversational product.
- No penalty, red state, zero score, or incomplete percentage for not-yet-covered curriculum.
- No grade claim without assessed-scope and evidence-strength qualifiers.
- No restored `/thinking-memory` route or standalone retained-chain library.
- No model answer, full chain, or reserved-paper content before an explicit, consequence-aware action.
- No runtime model dependency for browsing, recall launch, scope, progress, checklists, or transfer
  selection.
