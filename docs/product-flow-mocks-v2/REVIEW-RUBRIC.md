# Independent Concept Review Rubric

Use this rubric before combining ideas from the three concepts. Score the saved boards and written
flows, not the designer's explanation alone.

## Core Product Fit

| Criterion           | Question                                                                             | Weight |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | -----: |
| Immediate action    | Can Maya identify the next useful action within two seconds?                         |     15 |
| Question centrality | Is a real question more prominent than navigation, metrics, or product explanation?  |     12 |
| Journey continuity  | Is it obvious how home leads to a question, feedback, method, transfer, and return?  |     12 |
| Calm choice         | Does the concept reduce decisions without hiding essential control?                  |     10 |
| Partial curriculum  | Is not-yet-covered scope safe, editable, and excluded by default?                    |     10 |
| Evidence honesty    | Are assisted work, assessed scope, confidence, and grade range represented honestly? |     10 |
| Exam credibility    | Do metadata, typography, provenance, and paper mode feel trustworthy?                |      8 |
| Recall launch       | Can a useful short stack start with one or two decisions?                            |      6 |
| Reasoning transfer  | Does the method lead to a meaningfully different next question?                      |      6 |
| Progress depth      | Is high-level progress simple while detailed evidence remains inspectable?           |      5 |
| Mobile parity       | Does the mobile view preserve the same hierarchy and actions?                        |      3 |
| Visual character    | Does it feel distinctive without becoming decorative, childish, or corporate?        |      3 |

Total: 100.

## Failure Conditions

A concept cannot be recommended unchanged if any of these are true:

- the first screen presents equal-weight choices for every product feature;
- question lists are static and unaware of prior work;
- a large analytics surface appears before an obvious action;
- `not yet covered` is visually merged with weak performance;
- an assisted answer is presented as equivalent to an independent answer;
- an authentic reserved paper can be exposed without a clear warning;
- conversation becomes the main tutoring surface or blocks ordinary navigation;
- the design depends on reading a paragraph before clicking;
- fine text, decorative cards, or small controls make the image look polished but unusable;
- a question loses its authentic response format or source context.

## Inspection Checklist For Generated Boards

- Read every visible heading and primary action.
- Check whether a student could infer click targets without annotation arrows.
- Check desktop and mobile hierarchy independently.
- Look for invented statistics, incorrect official claims, or contradictory scope states.
- Look for repeated card grids that recreate the current browsing problem.
- Confirm that progress and grade information state their scope.
- Confirm that paper mode looks deliberately different from ordinary practice.
- Confirm that the same underlying state appears consistently across all screens.
- Treat mangled image-model text as a rendering defect; use `concept.md` to judge intended wording,
  but request a visual revision if the main hierarchy is no longer legible.
