# High-Fidelity iPad Art-Direction Brief

The earlier concept boards remain useful for journey logic, but they are wireframes. They are not the
visual target. This pass restarts the art direction around a full-screen iPad experience.

## The bar

The product should feel desirable before it explains itself: confident, modern, distinctive, and
made for touch. A skeptical Year 10 student should see a serious tool with cultural taste, not an
institutional portal, school LMS, administration system, or generic AI product.

These are screenshots of an operational product, not marketing pages or speculative campaign art.
Every composition must make sense as a screen a student can repeatedly use: clear navigation and
state, plausible touch controls, real answer entry, buildable layout, and restrained decoration.

Use:

- iPad landscape proportions, with every screen composed for 4:3;
- one magnetic focal object rather than a field of equal-weight panels;
- expressive typography with an intentional contrast between exam content and product interface;
- depth, layering, colour, illustration, or subtle spatial motifs when they help hierarchy;
- generous negative space and large touch targets;
- concise interface copy and immediately legible actions;
- a paper surface for an authentic question, treated as a designed object rather than a bordered
  form;
- motion implied through placement, cropping, progression, and transitions between states.

Avoid:

- public-service or government-site aesthetics;
- thin navy outlines around every object;
- repeated white cards on a pale background;
- dashboard grids, filter forms, settings walls, and dense navigation;
- tiny metadata pills or all-caps labels competing with the question;
- generic gradients used merely to signal AI;
- chat bubbles, assistant avatars, or a permanent prompt box;
- childish school imagery, mascots, confetti, XP, streaks, and game-map clichés;
- decorative constellations that make the question harder to read.
- oversized campaign headlines that replace navigation, state, or usable question controls;
- decorative scenes that imply an app but do not show how the student actually operates it.

## Product truths every direction must preserve

- Home opens on a finite trail: four questions, about ten minutes, and a real endpoint.
- The current question is the hero. Scope, provenance, and selection rationale remain available
  without dominating it.
- Authentic paper questions look and behave like exam material.
- After a weak answer, improving the complete response is the primary action.
- Transfer cannot begin until the improved answer passes recheck.
- Topics that have not yet been covered at school are excluded without penalty.
- A reserved authentic paper stays out of ordinary practice and cannot be exposed silently.
- Recall starts from a useful recommendation, not a configuration form.
- Progress qualifies the working range with assessed scope and evidence strength.
- Frequent actions use direct controls. Natural-language adjustment is summon-only and resolves to a
  structured plan rather than a conversation.

- Student: Maya, Year 10.
- Route: AQA GCSE Combined Science, Higher.
- Current subject: Biology.

- Later at school: Homeostasis and Response, Inheritance, Variation and Evolution, Ecology.
- Evidence: 14 checked questions; a recurring missing link from oxygen to aerobic respiration and

## Required visual sequence

Each independent direction should show at least four separate full-screen states:

1. Returning home: finite trail and an irresistible next question.
2. Attempt: source-faithful question and response surface.
3. Checked answer: evidence, one missing link, `Improve this answer`, and locked transfer.
4. Support destination: recall, progress, or reserved papers expressed in the same visual system.

The final synthesis may borrow different interaction ideas from the independent directions, but it
must have one coherent visual grammar rather than looking assembled from components.
