# Visual Direction Comparison

## Recommendation

Use **Living Paper Observatory** as the visual and spatial base. It is the best balance of distinctive,
beautiful, question-first, and operational. Its paper is a real working surface, and its progress map
turns curriculum evidence into something a student can understand without becoming a dashboard.

Borrow two specific pieces from the other directions:

- Electric Paper's explicit `Checked → Improve → Recheck → Transfer` state rail and its decisive
  recall launch.
- Afterglow Atlas's split answer workspace and protected-paper shelf, especially where dense source
  material makes a two-pane layout clearer.

Do not average all three visual styles. The synthesis should keep Living Paper's typography, colour,
paper material, and restrained spatial paths; the borrowed ideas are interaction patterns, not a
second or third skin.

## Concept One: Living Paper Observatory

Why it works:

- The question is visually precious without being a marketing banner.
- Paper remains the editable, tappable surface in every state.
- The finite trail is visible but not game-like.
- The checked state asks one useful question and keeps transfer locked.
- The evidence map handles `14 checked`, `3 of 4 taught topics`, a cautious `6–8` range, and later
  topics in one coherent composition.
- The recall screen removes almost every configuration decision while retaining direct control.

Guardrails for implementation:

- Build the background with restrained CSS/SVG layers; no video or constant particle animation.
- Keep paper grain subtle enough for long reading.
- Make spatial paths functional and selectable, never ambient decoration behind text.
- Provide a calm light option if sustained dark-shell use tests poorly.

## Concept Two: Electric Paper

Why it works:

- It has the strongest personality and will not be mistaken for a school portal.
- The home was revised from a poster-like composition into a genuine four-item app workspace.
- The answer-check sequence is the easiest to read across time.
- The passed-recheck screen makes the transfer unlock feel earned.
- Its recall launch makes seven due cards feel immediate without exposing a settings wall.

Guardrails for implementation:

- Use the condensed display face only for short state phrases, never long questions or typed answers.
- Reduce the number of registration marks and paper effects in ordinary daily use.
- Keep huge editorial statements out of utility destinations unless they clarify the state.
- Do not let acid lime become a reward colour or imply that activity equals mastery.

## Concept Three: Afterglow Atlas

Why it works:

- Every screen is obviously buildable and touchable.
- The two-pane attempt keeps authentic content and writing simultaneously visible.
- Feedback quotes the student's own answer and converts omissions into questions rather than copy.
- The transfer preview is explicit, attributable, and locked.
- The paper shelf makes an untouched June 2024 mock materially different from exposed papers.
- Recall is polished and minimal without losing subject and scope control.

Guardrails for implementation:

- Avoid glowing controls on every surface; reserve cobalt for the single next action.
- Keep the dark answer pane from feeling like a chat composer.
- Let source material determine the split ratio rather than fixing every question at 50/50.
- Use the constellation thread sparingly so it stays meaningful.
