# Visual QA

Score every generated screen against these questions before it reaches review.

## First-glance test

- Is there one unmistakable focal point within one second?
- Does it look like a designed iPad product rather than a website scaled up?
- Does it look like an operational product rather than a marketing page or campaign poster?
- Would the screen still be recognisable with the logo removed?
- Is the visual identity credible for a 14–16-year-old without looking juvenile?
- Is there something visually memorable beyond a colour applied to ordinary cards?

## Composition

- Is the full 4:3 canvas used intentionally?
- Does the screen avoid a header-plus-card-grid template?
- Are touch targets comfortably sized without turning into a wall of buttons?
- Does the eye move naturally from question to answer area to primary action?
- Is secondary information disclosed spatially rather than boxed equally?

## Typography and colour

- Is exam copy distinct from interface copy without becoming a fake worksheet?
- Are there at most two strong type voices and a disciplined scale?
- Does colour create hierarchy or emotion instead of decorating borders?
- Is contrast sufficient across the entire screen?
- Are source details readable but quieter than the prompt?

## Flow truth

- Is provenance accurate and adjacent to the question?
- Is `Improve this answer` primary after a missing reasoning link?
- Is transfer visibly unavailable until the improved response passes recheck?
- Is a reserved paper protected?
- Are later-at-school topics neutral rather than treated as failure?
- Is there one clear next action rather than an unexplained choice set?

## Rejection conditions

Regenerate or edit the screen if any of these are true:

- it resembles a government, banking, analytics, or school-admin portal;
- the design is primarily thin outlines, white rectangles, and navy text;
- the question is not the largest meaningful object;
- the UI contains garbled or fabricated source information;
- a decorative motif compromises reading or answer entry;
- the layout relies on a desktop sidebar or tiny pointer targets;
- it looks like a generic chatbot, a streak app, or a children’s game;
- it is a collage of several mini-screens instead of one usable iPad state.
- its visual drama comes at the cost of plausible navigation, touch interaction, or answer entry.
