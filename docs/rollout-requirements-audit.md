## Independent gap findings in this release

- The recovered request asked for a predicted grade, but arbitrary independently checked practice
  questions do not share the sampling or total-mark basis of a GCSE award. The product research
  gate also forbids shipping a grade conversion before held-out human-marked and whole-paper
  calibration. This release therefore reports the exact checked-answer mark rate, assessed and
  unassessed scope, and evidence strength without calling it a GCSE grade. Compact June 2024 AQA
  and OCR boundary metadata is retained as offering-specific context only: Combined Science
  requires all six papers and a double award, Foundation ceilings remain explicit, the 2027
  Computer Science specification cannot use a 2024 legacy boundary, and History abstains without
  an exact option route.
- The previous standard study-card releases were valid reviewed starters, but three or four cards per top-level chapter did not satisfy broad descendant coverage. The additive descendant plan measures every eligible official section/topic descendant, excludes Higher-only content from shared Foundation/Higher releases, and shards the exact uncovered identities at no more than 20 cards.
- The interrupted descendant queue had 35 jobs. Exactly 12 were durably recorded as accepted before interruption; those 12 contain 82 independently accepted cards across 42 offering/topic coverage rows. Their generator and reviewer rollout evidence is recovered without model calls under explicit `rollout-recovered-v1` release ids. Raw session JSONL is deliberately excluded from release commits; compact evidence retains its SHA-256, nonzero sanitized row count and exact prompt/model-output hashes. One accepted Chemistry card retains its separate repair generator/reviewer lineage, and the two Physics Momentum cards retain the later target-only tier correction. Failed, running and queued jobs are not relabelled as accepted.
- The failed Combined Physics trace contained 12 independently accepted cards and three rejected cards. The 12 acceptances are now an explicit 12-card immutable partial release; the planner moved from 339/450 to 351/438 covered/uncovered. The rejected stopping-distance target remains shared Foundation/Higher scope, while the two momentum targets remain Higher-only. The prepared execution queue proves a 438-target unique union with zero accepted-target overlap and converts 23 logical lineages into 26 tier-safe physical batches.
- The accepted 67-card Literature release was lawful but shallow: two plot events per option and two quotations per reusable public-domain work. The additive plan contributes 96 ordered plot cards, 72 exact lawful quotations and three poetry quote-to-analysis bridges while preserving explicit modern-quotation and anthology-completeness withholding.
- The exact completion runner is hash-bound (lock fingerprint `60f63783f3ba87b3f631d5409f88039ad1d3e804c79dce4edf39deb768bb603c`) and dry-passed without creating execution state or invoking a model. It runs 24 future `standard-study-card-descendant-coverage-v2` three-or-four-choice batches plus the two archived v1 Combined Physics review-only outputs as 25 atomic processes at concurrency no greater than two, then requires planner remainder zero before starting the 13 Literature shards. The two recovery outputs share one execution group, so their identical command is never launched twice; the 24 accepted baseline releases remain unchanged and have zero target overlap with the 438-target queue.
- A shared content-addressed lineage gate now rejects a completion artifact unless every declared generator and independent reviewer is an exact passed `gpt-5.6-sol`/`max` run with distinct cross-role ids; every summary, prompt and model output matches its retained SHA-256; event evidence has a raw-stream SHA-256 and nonzero sanitized row count; and every accepted card's learner-visible semantic hash occurs in both generator output and the exact input to an accepting reviewer. Only deterministic catalog/source/offering binding and normalized choice keys are explicit post-review exclusions. Four reconstructed baseline releases already carry this manifest. The prepared runner materializes it for all 26 + 13 future outputs, and the final verifier also locks the official topology to exactly 1,401 offering/component pairs and 152 selectable deck scopes.
- `scripts/lib/recall-card-generation-prompt.mjs`, `scripts/lib/recall-card-bundle.mjs`, the `generate:recall-cards` command, the runtime recall catalog and the former D1 exact-four trigger showed that the legacy recall compiler remained active. The release resolves that gap prospectively: accepted v5–v9 bundles retain exact-four validation, v10 accepts 3–4, and migration `0023` replaces the publication trigger without rewriting accepted releases.
- The recovered paper-import request is selective stronger-model reprocessing of roughly 20 representative papers, not a blanket reimport of all historical papers.

## Pre-release production baseline (2026-07-16)

- Question D1: 2,426 questions from 181 imported papers. Distribution was
  Biology 1, Combined Science 41, Computer Science 6, English Language 20,
  English Literature 19, Geography 15 and History 79; no Separate Chemistry or
  Physics papers were imported.
- Curriculum D1: 10 official specifications, 17 offerings and 979 components.
  Question mappings were complete for Geography/History/English Language,
  partial for science/Literature, and zero for all 228 Computer Science questions.
- Recall D1: 46 rows total, of which 31 were published and 15 retired. Published
  coverage was Biology 14, Chemistry 8 and Physics 9, each in a single Combined
  Higher topic. The checked-in separate-Higher fallback had 76 cards (24/23/29)
  and no Foundation, Combined fallback or non-science coverage.
- English: 145 of 255 Literature questions depended on an extract, but nearly
  all lacked usable source text; 47 lacked a reviewed model answer. Language had
  similar source-context gaps. Solvability eligibility did not quarantine them.
- Paper-taking: the paper renderer was read-only; the public past-paper surface
  was a download list.
- Learner model: response time existed in evidence, but predicted grade was a
  hard-coded null and there was no home-level resume action.
